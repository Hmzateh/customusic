How Once Human builds work

A "build" isn't just your weapon. It's three things working together:

  • Specialization: your class archetype and core abilities
  • Weapons: primary and secondary, each with their own scaling
  • Memetics: passive and active modifiers that shape how your abilities behave

Most new players focus only on weapons. That's a mistake. Your specialization matters more. It defines your playstyle, your damage, your survivability. Weapons are the tool. The specialization is the engine.

Good news: you can swap specializations without losing progress. One of the best design decisions in the game. Don't feel locked in.

Shrapnel build (the current meta)

This is the one everyone runs. Shrapnel deals massive burst damage in a wide area. It shreds groups and hits hard on single targets too.

Why it works

Shrapnel scales off explosive damage modifiers and benefits from the right memetic setup. Stack the right passives and each burst clears entire packs in raidzones. Other builds need twice the time.

Best weapons for shrapnel

  • Primary: TEC-9 or any high-RPM SMG. Faster proc rate means higher DPS.
  • Secondary: Shotgun for close encounters when burst is on cooldown.

Key memetics

  • Area damage increase
  • Cooldown reduction. Shorter gaps between bursts = more overall damage.
  • Crit modifiers that stack with explosive scaling

Out of all Once Human builds, this is the best PvE option right now. If you only learn one, make it shrapnel.

Frost vortex build (the control pick)

Frost Vortex doesn't hit as hard as Shrapnel on paper. But it does something Shrapnel can't: crowd control. Enemies get slowed, frozen, grouped together. In dense raidzones, that control is worth more than raw damage.

Why it works

The vortex pulls enemies into a cluster while applying frost stacks. Once frozen, your follow-up damage is amplified. Group them, then burst. When it clicks, it feels incredible.

Best weapons for frost vortex

  • Primary: Assault rifle with steady DPS to capitalize on frozen targets.
  • Secondary: Sniper rifle for picking off frozen elites at range.

Key memetics

  • Frost duration extenders
  • Damage amplification on frozen/slowed targets
  • Pull radius increase so the vortex catches more enemies

Best pick for group play. A Frost Vortex user makes everyone else's job easier.

Bounce build (the sleeper pick)

Bounce doesn't get the attention it deserves. Projectiles ricochet between enemies. In tight corridors or dense groups, the damage gets absurd. Tier lists underrate it because it's inconsistent in open areas. But in the right scenarios, nothing beats it.

Why it works

Each bounce maintains or increases damage depending on your memetics. In a room with six enemies packed together, a single shot can hit four or five of them. The math gets silly fast.

Best weapons for bounce

  • Primary: Pistol or SMG. Faster fire rate means more bouncing projectiles in the air at once.
  • Secondary: Anything with range for open-world cleanup.

Key memetics

  • Bounce count increase. More bounces = more damage.
  • Projectile damage scaling
  • Ricochet damage retention so each bounce doesn't lose damage

High-reward gameplay. Not the most consistent of the Once Human builds, but when the geometry lines up, nothing else comes close.

Starfall inversion build

Starfall Inversion is the "big hit" build. Instead of sustained damage, you drop massive single blows that delete priority targets. Think of it as the sniper equivalent of specializations.

Best weapons: Sniper rifle or marksman rifle. High damage per shot matches the burst nature of the specialization.

The catch: It's mechanically demanding. You need to time abilities around damage windows. Miss your window and your DPS craters until cooldowns reset. High ceiling, but a high floor too.

Power surge build

Power Surge is the sustained damage option. No big burst windows. No flashy mechanics. Just consistent, reliable damage over time.

Best weapons: Assault rifle. The steady damage profile matches the specialization perfectly.

Not going to top any damage leaderboards, but it never feels bad either. Solid B+ in everything. Great for solo players who want reliability.

Fast gunner build

Fast Gunner means high rate of fire, mobility, hit-and-run tactics. It's fun. It's also not great in endgame content where enemies have massive health pools.

Best weapons: Dual SMGs or any fast-firing automatic. Speed is the whole identity.

Great for open-world exploration and casual PvE. Falls off in late-game raidzones. If you're pushing endgame, treat this as a stepping stone.

Best deviants to pair with your build

Your build doesn't exist in a vacuum. The Deviants you assign to your loadout matter. Most guides ignore this.

  • DPS builds (Shrapnel, Bounce): Use Deviants that buff damage or debuff enemy resistances.
  • Control builds (Frost Vortex): Use Deviants that extend crowd control or add secondary CC.
  • Sustained builds (Power Surge, Fast Gunner): Use Deviants with passive healing or resource regeneration.

Specific Deviants rotate with patches. The principle stays the same: match your Deviant to your build's identity. Getting this right is what separates decent Once Human builds from great ones.

FAQ

What is the best build in Once Human right now?

Shrapnel. Highest DPS in PvE, works well in raidzones, doesn't require perfect execution. If you only learn one, make it this one.

What is the best class in Once Human?

Once Human uses specializations instead of traditional classes. You can swap them freely. Best specialization for PvE is Shrapnel. For a tactical style, Frost Vortex is a close second.

What is the best memetic specialization?

Shrapnel with area damage and cooldown reduction memetics is the strongest setup. Frost Vortex with freeze duration memetics is best for group utility.

Can you change your build in Once Human?

Yes. Specializations can be swapped without losing progress. Your weapons and memetics stay in your inventory. Experimentation is completely free.

What are the best weapons in Once Human?

TEC-9 and high-RPM SMGs dominate for proc-based builds like Shrapnel and Bounce. Assault rifles are the best all-rounders. Sniper rifles are essential for Starfall Inversion. Check the official Steam page for the latest weapon balance changes.
