Once Human has a build problem. Not because the system is bad — it's actually one of the deeper ones in the survival genre right now — but because the game does a terrible job explaining it. You pick a specialization, grab some weapons, slot in some memetics, and hope for the best. Most players figure out twenty hours in that their build is a mess.

So here's a proper breakdown. Every meta build worth running on PC right now, what makes them work, and which ones are a trap. If you're still deciding whether the game is worth your time, check out our Once Human review first.

How builds actually work

A "build" in Once Human isn't just your weapon. It's the combination of three things:

  • Specialization — your class archetype, which defines your core abilities
  • Weapons — your primary and secondary, each with their own scaling
  • Memetics — passive and active modifiers that change how your abilities behave

The mistake most new players make is focusing only on weapons. Your specialization matters more. It shapes your entire playstyle, your damage output, your survivability. Weapons are the tool. The specialization is the engine.

You can swap specializations without losing progress, which is one of the best design decisions in the game. Don't feel locked in. Try things.

Best specializations right now

Not all specializations are equal. Some are clearly ahead of the pack, and the meta has shifted a lot since launch. Here's where things stand in 2026:

S-tier:

  • Shrapnel
  • Frost Vortex

A-tier:

  • Bounce
  • Starfall Inversion
  • Power Surge

B-tier:

  • Fast Gunner
  • Pathfinder

The gap between S and A isn't massive, but in high-level raidzones it starts to show. B-tier builds work fine for general content, but they fall behind in endgame scenarios where damage windows are tight.

Shrapnel build — the current meta

This is the one everyone runs, and for good reason. Shrapnel deals massive burst damage in a wide area. It shreds through groups of enemies and hits hard on single targets too.

Why it works

The shrapnel specialization scales off explosive damage modifiers and benefits heavily from the right memetic setup. When you stack the right passives, each shrapnel burst can clear entire packs in raidzones that other builds need twice the time to handle.

Best weapons for shrapnel

  • Primary: TEC-9 or any high-RPM SMG — the faster you proc shrapnel effects, the higher your DPS
  • Secondary: Shotgun for close encounters when the burst is on cooldown

Key memetics

  • Anything that increases area damage
  • Cooldown reduction is huge here — shorter gaps between bursts means more overall damage
  • Crit modifiers stack well with the explosive scaling

Who it's for: Players who want the highest damage output and don't mind being in medium range. It's the best PvE build in the game right now, period.

Frost vortex build — the control pick

Frost Vortex doesn't hit as hard as Shrapnel on paper, but it does something Shrapnel can't: crowd control. Enemies get slowed, frozen, grouped together. In raidzones with dense mob waves, that control is worth more than raw damage.

Why it works

The vortex pulls enemies into a cluster while applying frost stacks. Once they're frozen, your follow-up damage is amplified. It's a two-step process — group, then burst — and when it clicks, it's incredibly satisfying.

Best weapons for frost vortex

  • Primary: Assault rifle with steady DPS to capitalize on frozen targets
  • Secondary: Sniper rifle for picking off frozen elites at range

Key memetics

  • Frost duration extenders
  • Damage amplification on frozen/slowed targets
  • Pull radius increase (makes the vortex catch more enemies)

Who it's for: Players who like a more tactical approach. You're controlling the fight, not just racing to kill everything first. In group play, a Frost Vortex user makes everyone else's job easier.

Bounce build — the sleeper pick

Bounce doesn't get the attention it deserves. The projectiles ricochet between enemies, and in tight corridors or dense groups the damage gets absurd. It's inconsistent in open areas, which is why tier lists tend to underrate it, but in the right scenarios it outperforms everything.

Why it works

Each bounce maintains or increases damage depending on your memetic setup. In a room with six enemies packed together, a single shot can hit four or five of them. The math gets silly fast.

Best weapons for bounce

  • Primary: Pistol or SMG — faster fire rate means more bouncing projectiles in the air at once
  • Secondary: Anything with range for open-world cleanup

Key memetics

  • Bounce count increase (critical — the more bounces, the more damage)
  • Projectile damage scaling
  • Ricochet damage retention (so each bounce doesn't lose damage)

Who it's for: Players who like chaotic, high-reward gameplay. It's not consistent, but when the geometry lines up, nothing else comes close.

Starfall inversion build

Starfall Inversion is the "big hit" build. Instead of sustained damage, you're dropping massive single blows that delete priority targets. Think of it as the sniper equivalent of specializations.

Best weapons: Sniper rifle or marksman rifle. You want high damage per shot to match the burst nature of the specialization.

The catch: It's mechanically demanding. You need to time your abilities around damage windows, and if you miss, your DPS craters until cooldowns reset. High ceiling, higher floor than most players expect.

Power surge build

Power Surge is the sustained damage option. No big burst windows, no flashy mechanics. You just deal consistent, reliable damage over time. It's the "I don't want to think too hard" build, and that's not an insult.

Best weapons: Assault rifle. The steady damage profile matches the specialization perfectly.

Who it's for: Players who want something reliable for solo play. It's not going to top any damage leaderboards, but it never feels bad either. Solid B+ in everything.

Fast gunner build

Fast Gunner is exactly what it sounds like — high rate of fire, mobility, hit-and-run tactics. It's fun. It's also not great in endgame content where enemies have massive health pools and mobility matters less than raw output.

Best weapons: Dual SMGs or any fast-firing automatic. Speed is the whole identity.

Honest take: Great for open-world exploration and casual PvE. Falls off hard in late-game raidzones. If you're planning to push endgame, treat this as a stepping stone, not a destination.

Best deviants to pair with your build

Your build doesn't exist in a vacuum. The Deviants you assign to your loadout matter, and most guides ignore this completely.

A few principles:

  • DPS builds (Shrapnel, Bounce): Use Deviants that buff your damage or debuff enemy resistances. Anything that adds damage amplification stacks with your burst.
  • Control builds (Frost Vortex): Use Deviants that extend crowd control duration or add secondary CC effects.
  • Sustained builds (Power Surge, Fast Gunner): Use Deviants that provide passive healing or resource regeneration. You're in fights longer, so survivability matters more.

The specific Deviants rotate with patches, but the principle stays the same. Match your Deviant to your build's identity, don't just pick the one with the highest rarity.

Best weapons tier list

Don't sleep on weapon mods. A modded blue weapon can outperform an unmodded legendary. Mods matter more than rarity in most cases.

FAQ

What is the best build in Once Human right now?

Shrapnel. It has the highest DPS in PvE, works well in raidzones, and doesn't require perfect execution to be effective. If you're only going to learn one build, make it this one.

What is the best class in Once Human?

Once Human doesn't use traditional classes — it uses specializations, which function similarly but can be swapped freely. The best specialization for PvE is Shrapnel. For a more tactical style, Frost Vortex is a close second.

What is the best memetic specialization?

Shrapnel with area damage and cooldown reduction memetics is the strongest overall setup. Frost Vortex with freeze duration memetics is the best for group utility.

Can you change your build in Once Human?

Yes. Specializations can be swapped without losing progress. Your weapons and memetics stay in your inventory. This is one of the best things about the game — experimentation is free.

What are the best weapons in Once Human?

The TEC-9 and high-RPM SMGs dominate for builds that rely on proc rates (Shrapnel, Bounce). Assault rifles are the best all-rounders. Sniper rifles are niche but essential for Starfall Inversion.
