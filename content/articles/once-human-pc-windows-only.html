What is Once Human?

Once Human is a free-to-play multiplayer survival game set in a post-apocalyptic world contaminated by Stardust, an alien substance that mutated everything it touched. Creatures, landscapes, even some survivors. You play as a Meta-Human, one of the few who can resist the contamination and wield its power.

The gameplay loop mixes several genres together:

  • Survival: manage hunger, thirst, and health in a hostile world
  • Crafting: build weapons, tools, and gear through the Tech system (formerly called Memetics)
  • Base building: claim territory, build structures, and defend them
  • Open world exploration: dungeons, world bosses, hidden secrets across a massive map
  • PvE and PvP: depending on which server type you pick

What really sets Once Human apart from other survival games is the Deviation system. Deviations are mutant creatures you can capture and tame. Combat Deviations fight alongside you. Territory Deviations passively farm resources while you explore. Crafting Deviations unlock exclusive recipes. Think of them as post-apocalyptic companions that actually pull their weight.

Is Once Human free to play?

Yes. Once Human is completely free on PC. No hidden costs, no trial period. The monetization model relies on cosmetic microtransactions: skins, outfits, and base decorations. None of them give any gameplay advantage.

There's also a seasonal Battle Pass with both a free and a premium track. The premium version costs money but only unlocks cosmetic rewards. Once Human is not pay-to-win in any way.

How to download Once Human on PC

Three options, all free:

  • Steam: search for "Once Human", hit Play, and wait for the 55 GB download. Easiest option
  • Epic Games Store: same process, click Get
  • Loading Bay: NetEase's own launcher. Less popular, works just fine

Your progress is tied to your account, not the launcher. You can switch from Steam to Epic without losing anything.

Best Once Human builds in 2026

The version 2.3.6 update changed everything. Level-gated unlocks are gone, replaced by exploration-driven progression. Your build choices matter from the first hour now.

Shrapnel build (best PvE)

The strongest PvE build right now. Three Shrapnel fragments deal 55% of your weapon damage each, and the whole setup revolves around maximizing crit chance. Great Ones and dungeon bosses melt fast. If you like seeing big damage numbers, this is your pick.

Power Surge build (best all-rounder)

Less technical, surprisingly strong. It relies on elemental effects stacking over time. Perfect if you don't want to min-max every stat and just want something that works in PvE and PvP.

Current meta gear

  • Primary weapon: SOCR (top-tier assault rifle) or Div-Evo for faster gameplay
  • Secondary weapon: Recurve Crossbow for the Bull's Eye debuff
  • PvE armor: 4-piece Falcon set, reliable at every stage of the game
  • PvP armor: Lonewolf set, with scaling CRIT rate and CRIT damage bonuses that ramp up during combat

5 beginner tips for Once Human on PC

A few things that'll save you hours of frustration early on.

  1. Start on a PvE server. Once Human has a lot of systems to learn. PvE lets you figure them out without getting raided every night
  2. Farm wood first. Every tree gives 30 to 50 logs. You need them for campfires, basic tools, walls, and furniture. Wood is the most demanded resource in the early game
  3. Pick your base location carefully. Set up near ore deposits and world event spawn points. A good location saves you hours of travel across the entire season
  4. Capture Deviations early. Territory Deviations collect resources on their own while you explore. For free. Don't sleep on them
  5. Explore everything. Since the 2.3.6 update, progression is tied to exploration, not levels. The more zones you discover, the more Tech you unlock
